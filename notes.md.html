# Computational AdS/CFT notes ## Defining the metric Suppose we have a metric $g_{\mu\nu}$. * $g^{\mu\nu}$ is then simply the inverse of $g_{\mu\nu}$. * \begin{equation} \Gamma_{\lambda\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{2}\left( \partial_\nu g_{\lambda\mu} + \partial_\mu g_{\lambda\mu} - \partial_\lambda g_{\mu\nu} \right) \label{eq:Gamma-ddd} \end{equation} * \begin{equation} \Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\nu} = g^{\lambda\rho} \Gamma_{\rho\mu\nu} \label{eq:Gamma-Udd} \end{equation} * \begin{equation} \partial_\rho \Gamma_{\lambda\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{2}\left( \partial_\rho \partial_\nu g_{\lambda\mu} + \partial_\rho \partial_\mu g_{\lambda\mu} - \partial_\rho \partial_\lambda g_{\mu\nu} \right) \end{equation} * \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \partial_\kappa \Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\nu} &= \partial_\kappa \left( g^{\lambda\rho} \Gamma_{\rho\mu\nu} \right) \\ &= \left( \partial_\kappa g^{\lambda\rho}\right) \Gamma_{\rho\mu\nu} + g^{\lambda\rho} \left( \partial_\kappa \Gamma_{\rho\mu\nu} \right) \\ &= \left( \partial_\kappa g^{\lambda\rho} \right) \Gamma_{\rho\mu\nu} + \frac{1}{2} g^{\lambda\rho}\left( \partial_\kappa \partial_\nu g_{\rho\mu} + \partial_\kappa \partial_\mu g_{\rho\mu} - \partial_\kappa \partial_\rho g_{\mu\nu} \right) \end{aligned} \end{equation} * Covariant derivative: \begin{equation} \nabla_\mu T_\nu = \partial_\mu T_\nu - \Gamma^\lambda_{\nu\mu} T_\lambda \end{equation} ### Maxwell equations Assume that we have access to $A_\nu$ as well as $\partial_\mu A_\nu$. Then \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} & F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu \\ & F^\mu_\nu = g^{\mu\rho} F_{\rho\nu} \\ & F^{\mu\nu} = g^{\rho\nu} F^\mu_\rho \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation} And the corresponding equations of motions are \begin{equation} \mathtt{EOM\_Maxwell}_\nu = \nabla_\mu F^\mu_\nu = 0 \end{equation} We compute it as \begin{equation} \nabla_\mu F^\mu_\nu = \partial_\mu F^\mu_\nu + \Gamma^\mu_{\lambda\mu} F^\lambda_\nu - \Gamma^\lambda_{\nu\mu} F^\mu_\lambda \end{equation} \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \mathtt{EOM\_Maxwell}_\nu &= \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \nabla_\mu F^\mu_\nu \\ &= \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \partial_\mu F^\mu_\nu + \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \left( \Gamma^\mu_{\lambda\mu} F^\lambda_\nu \right) - \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \left( \Gamma^\lambda_{\nu\mu} F^\mu_\lambda \right) \\ &= \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \partial_\mu F^\mu_\nu + \left( \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \Gamma^\mu_{\lambda\mu}\right) F^\lambda_\nu + \Gamma^\mu_{\lambda\mu} \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} F^\lambda_\nu \\ & - \left( \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} \Gamma^\lambda_{\nu\mu}\right) F^\mu_\lambda - \Gamma^\lambda_{\nu\mu} \frac{\delta}{\delta Q} F^\mu_\lambda \end{aligned} \end{equation} \begin{equation} \delta \Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\nu} = \left( \delta g^{\lambda\rho} \right) \Gamma_{\rho\mu\nu} + \frac{1}{2} g^{\lambda\rho}\left( \delta \partial_\nu g_{\rho\mu} + \delta \partial_\mu g_{\rho\mu} - \delta \partial_\rho g_{\mu\nu} \right) \end{equation} $\delta g_{\mu\nu}$, $\delta g^{\mu\nu}$, $\delta \partial_\lambda g_{\mu\nu}$, and $\delta \partial_\kappa \partial_\lambda g_{\mu\nu}$ are computed by Mathematica.
